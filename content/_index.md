The Computational Pathology and Spatially-Integrated Omics group develops computational methods to study tissue biology and support clinical translation.

Our work focuses on the analysis of **digitized tissue samples**, with the aim of **understanding how cells are organized within their tissue environment and how this organization relates to disease**. While much of our research is centred on cancer, our approaches are designed to be applicable across **different disease contexts** where tissue architecture, spatial biology, and clinical data can provide meaningful insights.

Being embedded within **Gemelli IRCCS Hospital** allows the group to work closely with pathologists, oncologists, radiologists, molecular biologists, surgeons, and other clinical and research professionals. This setting supports a direct connection between **computational method development** and **clinically relevant questions**, helping us design tools that are both technically robust and meaningful for real-world biomedical research and future clinical workflows.

The group’s activities are structured around three main directions:

1) The development of computational pathology algorithms and the discovery of digital biomarkers;

2) The integration of spatially resolved, multi-modal and multi-scale data, using pathology as a core;

3) The translation of AI-based tools toward routine research and clinical use.

Our main goal is to build **interpretable computational frameworks** that can be used to explain disease mechanisms and support precision medicine.
